The governance gap

Governments deploy systems.
Few govern them.

Municipalities, police departments, ports, defence establishments and sovereign governments deploy software constantly. But without accountability built into the architecture itself — compliance becomes a paper exercise, funds disappear between reporting layers, and citizens pay the price through failed services and unsafe cities.

₹1.87LCr

Lost annually to government system failures & inefficiency (India, World Bank estimate)

SonicLynk's answer: don't audit after. Build governance into the system so accountability is structural, not procedural. When every action is traceable, every officer is accountable, and every rupee is tracked — performance follows automatically.
