Why Captiv

Ownership over
delegation.

These are not HR taglines. They are the operating principles that shape what it actually feels like to work at Captiv — and why people who join us tend to stay.

Institutional work

Your work touches governments, banks and Fortune 500 enterprises. Not internal tools. Not demo apps. Not MVP experiments. Systems that run real operations and serve real people — in some cases, millions of them. When a Captiv system goes live, it matters.

Six engines

Cloud, fintech, BPM, government, AI, talent — six distinct practice areas under one company. This means breadth that lets you grow across domains without leaving. You can start in cloud infrastructure and move into AI, or from BPM into financial systems. The depth is there in every engine.

Global deployment

India, Africa, Middle East, Americas, Singapore. Your systems will be live in multiple countries, often within months of joining. The team you work with builds for contexts most technology companies never reach. This is what makes institutional breadth — not just enterprise scale.

Leaders build leaders

Captiv's operating principle. Every person is expected to own their domain, grow their capability and actively build the people around them. We don't wait for formal promotion cycles to give ownership — we give it at the beginning and hold people accountable to it. This is how institutional depth gets built.

What it's like to work here

Deep work.
Real accountability.

Captiv is not a company for people who want to be told what to do. It is a company for people who want to own a problem, drive it to completion and be accountable for the result. We operate on a principle of institutional depth — meaning we prefer fewer, deeper engagements over broad, shallow ones. The same principle applies internally: we prefer teams where every person is essential, not organisations padded with coordination layers.

Working at Captiv means exposure to the full system — not just the slice of it you're technically responsible for. Engineers understand why the business decision was made. Business analysts understand what the technical constraints actually are. Project managers understand both sides deeply enough to make decisions without escalation. This institutional intelligence is what separates people who join Captiv from people who accumulate years of narrow experience elsewhere.

Chennai is our home base. Most of our engineering, product, delivery and operations capability is built here — but our systems run globally. This means the work you do in Chennai has direct consequence in national ports, central banks and Fortune 500 operations around the world. That's the weight of it. And for the right person, that weight is the point.

What you build

Systems that governments, banks and Fortune 500 enterprises run their operations on. Not prototypes. Not pilots. Production infrastructure with real consequences.

How you grow

By taking ownership earlier than you expect, by working across six domains with real depth, and by being expected to build the people around you — not just yourself.

What we look for

Ownership mindset. Capability over credentials. People who find institutional complexity interesting, not frustrating. And people who are honest about what they don't know — and serious about closing the gap.

Where we are

Chennai (primary) · Remote-possible for select roles · Occasional travel to client sites in India, Africa and the Middle East
