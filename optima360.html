Where Optima 360 sits in the landscape

One name from each category.
Here is how we fit.

The low-code market has landmark names — Appian, Mendix, Microsoft Power Apps, OutSystems. Each dominates a distinct corner: enterprise BPM, composable apps, Microsoft-native automation, web-app delivery. Optima 360 does not compete with all of them everywhere. Here is an honest map: which category each name owns, where we complement them, and where the operated-delivery model we offer is genuinely different.

Captiv Optima 360 Category benchmark

The four names above are all self-serve platforms — you licence the product, then your team (or an SI partner) builds and runs the system. Optima 360 is in the operated quadrant: Captiv configures, delivers, and operates the system. That is not a positioning claim. It is a fundamentally different commercial model, and it is why flexible pricing and one-call access are possible.

The honest read The platforms above are all larger than Captiv. Appian and Microsoft have more engineers and longer track records in the low-code space. None of that determines which is the right answer for a given institution. The relevant question is not which platform is biggest — it is who delivers the outcome and stays accountable for it. Optima 360 is explicitly on the operated side of that question. When an institution needs a self-serve product with an SI partner to build it, Appian or Mendix is the right answer and we will say so.
